Charles Leclerc at the Bahrain Grand Prix
Sunday, March 5

Charles Leclerc's hopes for a podium finish were dashed at the Bahrain Grand Prix as his Ferrari SF-23 suffered an engine-related issue that forced him to retire from the race on Lap 40. Despite replacing the control electronics and energy store components of his power unit before the race, Leclerc still experienced an engine failure that may result in a grid penalty during the 2023 season. Ferrari will now have to analyze the data from the engine failure to prevent any future mishaps as they prepare for the upcoming Saudi Arabian Grand Prix.
Bahrain Grand Prix 2023
Sunday, March 5

Red Bull dominated the Bahrain Grand Prix to start their Formula One championship defence in emphatic style. Max Verstappen put a near 40-second gap between himself and the closest non-Red Bull car, while Red Bull’s cost cap penalty hasn’t hurt them, at least not yet. Meanwhile, Aston Martin’s podium finish with Fernando Alonso and P6 with Lance Stroll indicates the team is part of the big four teams, and the F1 midfield is looking to be a tighter battle than before.
